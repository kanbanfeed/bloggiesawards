The Glass Onion Award
2026 Recipient Oracle Corporation
"We thank you for your dedication, hard work, and the impact you have made."
↓
30,000 employees received a 6 AM email. No call. No manager. No warning. System access revoked immediately.
The Email Sent at 6 AM local time, signed by "Oracle Leadership" — no named executive. Employees in the US, India, Canada, and Mexico received identical termination notices simultaneously.
The Profit Oracle reported a 95% increase in net income last quarter — over $6 billion. Revenue reached $17.2 billion, up 22%. The company's remaining performance obligations hit $553 billion.
The Spend The cash freed by cutting 30,000 jobs — estimated at $8–10 billion annually — is being redirected to AI data center buildouts. Oracle has taken on $58 billion in new debt in two months.
The Timing Within weeks of the layoffs, Oracle announced a new CFO with a $950K salary, $2.5M bonus target, and a $26 million equity grant. Larry Ellison remains among the world's three wealthiest people.

Voices from the Ground

"No call. No meeting. No manager. No warning. Just an email. And a DocuSign. That's it. That's how they did it. We have a 17-month-old at home." — Spouse of an affected Oracle employee, via LinkedIn
"After 34 — 33 of them great — years at Oracle, I join the 30,000 or so laid off today. Quite a shock." — Nina Lewis, former Security Alert Manager, 34-year Oracle veteran
"Oracle conducted a significant reduction in force that impacted some of the most talented, dedicated, and high-performing people I've had the privilege of working alongside." — Michael Shepherd, Oracle Senior Operations Manager, via LinkedIn
